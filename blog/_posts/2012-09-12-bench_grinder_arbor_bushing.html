--- layout: content title: A Bench Grinder Arbor Bushing ---

When replacing my grinder's wheels the other day I discovered that the flanges on the arbors were very cheap and inaccurate metal stampings. Because of this I couldn't get the grinder wheel balanced correctly. After messing about trying to balance them using shims for half an hour I threw in the towel and decided to make a new bushing/flange for the arbor.

The drawings and stock, along with the original part for comparison.

Facing the stock to prepare for drilling.

Drilling the hole for the arbor.

Turning down the outer diameter to that of the hole through the grinding wheel (1.000").

Facing the inside of the flange, and relieving the flange so that the pressure on the wheel is away from the mounting hole.

The bushing is grooved to indicate where the bandsaw should part it from the bar.

The bushing is parted off, re-chucked and ready for facing.

Facing complete.

The completed bushing next to the original part.

There was no particular goal with posting these photos. It's a simple part but I like the way it turned out, so I thought I would post the photos here!
